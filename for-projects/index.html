The economics

Day-rate plus 20% of experience fees.

Of a typical $2,500 platform booking, the experience-fee component (everything outside accommodation and transport pass-through) is split 80/20 between platform operations and the conservation partner. The 20% share goes directly to your project.

On top of that, we pay a per-guest day-rate that compensates the time of the ranger or research lead who hosts each cohort. The day-rate is agreed up front and is paid for every confirmed booking, regardless of weather or last-minute changes.

For first-cohort partners, we waive our customary onboarding handling fee entirely. The first five bookings paid out to your programme on the most favourable terms we offer.

How partnership works.

Four steps, end-to-end. Designed to slot into existing programme operations, not disrupt them.

STEP 01

Intro call

Thirty minutes. We listen first. We learn what work is in season, what your capacity looks like, what would never work.

STEP 02

MOU and rate sheet

Simple two-page agreement. Day-rate, capacity cap, season windows, scope of work hosted.

STEP 03

Onboarding

Programme description, photo library, agreed briefing protocol. We do the heavy lifting on materials.

STEP 04

First cohorts

You receive your first scheduled cohort. We handle all guest comms, you handle the fieldwork.

Who does what.

We are not a volunteer-tourism platform. Travellers are paying guests, vetted, briefed, and there to be useful inside structured fieldwork. Your role is to lead the work, not to manage logistics.

We handle

  • Marketing and customer acquisition
  • Guest qualification and pre-trip briefing
  • Booking and payments
  • All travel logistics: flights advice, transfers, accommodation, meals
  • Mandatory insurance verification
  • Refund processing and dispute handling
  • Annual co-branded impact report
  • Day-of host expectations management

You handle

  • The conservation work itself, on your schedule
  • Briefing the visiting cohort on safety and method
  • Allocating ranger or research-lead time per booking
  • Confirming or declining each scheduled week
  • Reporting outputs back to us for the impact summary
  • Saying no to anything that does not fit your programme

Things partners ask.

What kind of guests are these?

Affluent, purpose-driven, from the US, UK, Europe, and Australia. Average age 35 to 65. Most have prior experience of luxury travel and are looking for a meaningful trip with a conservation outcome attached. We vet for fit before any booking is confirmed.

How many guests per week?

You define the cap. Most partners start at 2 to 4 guests per week and scale only if their team can absorb more. We never push the cap.

Are guests expected to do real work?

Yes, structured and supervised. Reef surveys, coral nursery support, mangrove planting, turtle monitoring depending on your programme. We brief them before arrival on what to expect and what NOT to expect. Realistic outcomes, not volunteer-tourism mythology.

What if a week does not work for the team?

You decline it. We will not confirm any booking on a week you have not opened. The calendar is built around your operations.

How are payouts handled?

Weekly reconciliation report by email. Payouts in USD via Wise or directly to your operating bank account. We can pay in SBD if your account is local. Currency conversion at the live mid-market rate.

Do you require a minimum commitment?

No. The MOU runs season by season. Either party can pause or end the partnership with 30 days notice ahead of the next season window. We are building for the long term, but we do not lock partners in.

What about insurance and liability?

Every guest carries comprehensive traveller insurance covering medical evacuation, verified by us at booking. Standard liability waivers are signed at the start of any in-water work. Indemnity language in the MOU is drafted to protect both sides.
