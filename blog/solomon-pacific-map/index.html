Field guide, Solomon Islands to French Polynesia

Marine restoration and premium-eco lodges across the Pacific, mapped.

Every verified premium-eco lodge and active marine restoration programme from the Solomon Islands east across Melanesia, Micronesia, and Polynesia all the way to French Polynesia. Vanuatu, Papua New Guinea, Fiji, New Caledonia, Northern Australia, Cook Islands, Tonga, Samoa, Niue, FSM, Palau, Marshall Islands, Kiribati, and Tahiti to the Marquesas. Primary sources cited for every pin.

The short version. A two-ocean atlas. Solomon Islands at the default centre, with Pacific neighbours east to French Polynesia, the Coral Triangle west across Indonesia and the Philippines, and the Indian Ocean reef belt covering the Maldives, Seychelles, Mauritius, Sri Lanka, Chagos / BIOT, Lakshadweep, Mozambique, Madagascar, and Tanzania / Zanzibar. Over 200 verified premium-eco lodges, 47 marine conservation areas including 11 UNESCO World Heritage and Biosphere sites, and 84 active restoration programmes. Gaps named honestly where no lodge sits within reach. Pan west to reach the Indian Ocean cluster.

How we chose what is on the map

Two filters. First: every conservation area and restoration programme is officially gazetted, named in IUCN, UNESCO, or government registries, or is a community-managed area with at least one peer-reviewed citation or NGO operational record. Second: every lodge is on an island or on a coastal site reachable by short boat or charter flight, and is independently rated 5-star or boutique-eco-premium by widely-cited travel publications, dive media, or government tourism boards.

The Solomon Islands tier of premium tourism is small and intentional. There are no 50-villa Aman properties in the Western Province, and that is the point. The lodges below are the ones that exist, that operate, and that meet the brief.

Honest gaps

  • East Rennell. UNESCO World Heritage Site, but no premium-eco lodge on the island. Charter flight from Honiara to Tigoa, then community guesthouses. Worth visiting; not yet served by the premium tier.
  • Choiseul Bay community MPAs. No premium lodge on Choiseul. Nearest premium-eco access is via Western Province lodges on chartered boats.
  • Reef Islands and Santa Cruz (Temotu). Some of the most undervisited reefs in the Pacific. No premium-eco lodge yet. Charter flights from Honiara required.
  • Malaita. Mai-Maasina Green Belt is significant but tourism infrastructure is limited.
  • Lamen Bay dugong sanctuary on Epi (Vanuatu). No premium lodge on Epi itself; access via inter-island flight from Port Vila.
  • Bismarck Sea (PNG, Manus, New Ireland). Conservation programmes exist; premium accommodation outside Walindi, Lissenung, Tufi, and Loloata is sparse.

Naming the gaps is part of the brief. The platform exists in part to develop premium-eco capacity in places that genuinely lack it.

Why this map exists

This map is the working reference behind the Solomon Islands Eco Tourism platform. It answers three operational questions at once. Where can a 5-star or boutique-eco traveller actually stay across the Solomons and the immediate Pacific? Which active marine conservation programmes can they meaningfully visit or support? And where do those two overlap inside an hour of road or boat travel?

It also names the gaps. There are island groups in the Solomons with significant conservation programmes and no premium accommodation within reach. Surfacing those is part of the platform's longer term mandate: helping owner-operators in the right places build the right kind of property.
