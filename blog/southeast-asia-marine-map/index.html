Field guide, Southeast Asia

Marine conservation projects and 5-star island hotels, mapped.

A working map of verified marine protected areas and 5-star island lodges across Southeast Asia, with primary sources cited for every pin. Useful as a planning reference, useful as a benchmark for what good looks like in the region.

The short version. 43 verified marine protected areas in green, 29 active marine restoration programmes in coral, and 53 verified 5-star island lodges in gold across Southeast Asia. Every restoration programme has been paired with at least one 5-star lodge within roughly an hour by road or boat where one exists. Where no 5-star lodge sits within an hour of a programme, the gap is named honestly. 78 primary sources cited end to end.

Marine conservation project / MPA Active restoration programme 5-star island lodge Tiles: OpenStreetMap contributors, ODbL

How we chose what is on the map

Two filters. First: every MPA on the map is officially gazetted, named in IUCN, UNESCO, or government registries, and has been operational for at least five years. Second: every lodge is independently rated 5-star or equivalent by widely-cited travel publications and is on an island or directly on the coast, never inland on the mainland.

Where a property has multiple sister-properties (for example, El Nido Resorts has four sister lodges in Palawan), we list the flagship only to keep the map readable.

Why this map exists

Three reasons. First, when we tell hotel and conservation partners we are building a premium-eco platform, the next question is always "show me what good looks like in the region." This map answers that.

Second, when prospective travellers ask whether premium-eco actually exists in the Coral Triangle, this is the answer. Twelve real lodges, twelve real MPAs, one map.

Third, the platform itself is anchored on the Solomon Islands, but the broader market is the Coral Triangle and its edges. Knowing what is already operating well next door is operationally useful.

What is missing from this map, and why

  • Solomon Islands and Papua New Guinea are deliberately omitted. Solomon Islands is the platform's home destination, covered in detail elsewhere on the site. PNG deserves its own map.
  • Maldives, Sri Lanka, Lakshadweep are not Southeast Asia, so are excluded by definition.
  • Sister-resort over-counting. El Nido Resorts has four properties in Palawan, all premium. We list Pangulasian only. Aman has multiple Pacific properties beyond Amanpulo, same treatment.
  • Many city-edge or coastal-but-not-island 5-star hotels exist. They are not on the map because the brief was islands directly on the sea, not mainland coastline.
Coordinate accuracy. Pin positions are accurate to roughly the nearest 0.1 degree of latitude and longitude, sufficient for visual placement on the map. Do not use these coordinates for navigation, anchoring, or any safety-critical purpose. For trip-planning purposes, contact the property or MPA management office directly to confirm the precise location, access route, and current operating status before travelling.
