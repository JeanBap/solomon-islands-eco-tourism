The destination

The Coral Triangle, almost untouched.

The Solomon Islands sit inside the most biodiverse marine region on Earth. Reef-fish diversity per square metre is among the highest in the world. Tourism arrivals are roughly 30,000 a year.

This is what travel looked like before mass-market tourism arrived. Empty lagoons, intact villages, lodges run by the families that have lived there for centuries. The conservation need is real, and the country actively wants premium low-impact travellers.

Questions

Things people ask before booking.

How long is each journey?

Most journeys run 4 to 7 days. Custom 10 to 14 day itineraries are available on request, including multi-island combinations.

Do I need to be a certified diver?

No. Most conservation work is shallow-water snorkel based and accessible to anyone comfortable in the water. Some itineraries offer optional dive add-ons for certified divers.

What does the price include?

Accommodation at a 5-star partner eco lodge, structured conservation work with a partner project, inter-island transport, and on-the-ground concierge. Flights to Honiara and traveller insurance are not included.

How is the money split?

On a typical $2,500 package, the platform retains 30%, the hotel margin is 12%, the conservation project receives 20% of experience fees, and the remainder pays accommodation and transport at cost. Full breakdown on every booking confirmation.

What is the cancellation policy?

60+ days before departure: 90% refund. 30 to 60 days: 50% refund. 15 to 30 days: 25% refund. Under 15 days: 0% refund except in cases of medical emergency or force majeure, where experience fees are refunded.

Is travel insurance required?

Yes. Proof of comprehensive traveller insurance covering medical evacuation is required at booking. We can recommend providers used by previous guests.

How do I get to the Solomon Islands?

Most guests fly into Honiara via Brisbane, Port Moresby, or Nadi. We coordinate inter-island transfers from Honiara to your lodge once you arrive.
