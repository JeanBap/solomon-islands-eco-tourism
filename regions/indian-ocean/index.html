Reference, Indian Ocean

The Indian Ocean reef belt.

Premium-eco lodges, marine protected areas, and verified restoration programmes across the Maldives, Seychelles, Mauritius, Sri Lanka, Chagos, Lakshadweep, Mozambique, Madagascar, and Tanzania.

Why this exists. The Solomon Islands platform is anchored on the Coral Triangle, but the same affluent purpose-driven traveller often visits the Indian Ocean within the same calendar year. This page is the working reference for what good looks like in the Indian Ocean reef belt: every premium-eco lodge that we have verified, every UNESCO and IUCN-recognised marine protected area, and every active restoration programme paired with a primary source.

The full interactive map covering both ocean basins is on the Indo-Pacific atlas page. This page summarises what is in the Indian Ocean section.

Maldives

Twenty-eight verified premium-eco lodges across nine atolls, plus the Hanifaru Bay UNESCO Biosphere Reserve and the largest concentration of resort-led coral restoration programmes in the world. Soneva Foundation, Reefscapers, Marine Savers, Manta Trust, the Maldives Whale Shark Research Programme, Olive Ridley Project, and Banyan Tree Marine Lab all run from Maldivian atolls.

See the interactive map for individual pin locations.

Seychelles

Seven premium-eco lodges across the Inner Islands plus the Aldabra Atoll UNESCO World Heritage Site (inscribed 1982) in the Outer Islands. Save Our Seas Foundation runs its global research HQ from Mahé, with a field research centre on D'Arros in the Amirantes. Islands Development Company runs ranger-supported restoration across the Outer Islands.

Mauritius and Rodrigues

Three flagship 5-star resorts (Constance Le Prince Maurice, One&Only Le Saint Géran, LUX* Le Morne). Le Morne Brabant is a UNESCO World Heritage cultural landscape with adjacent reef. Rodrigues hosts a community-managed South East Marine Protected Area. Mauritian Wildlife Foundation and Reef Conservation Mauritius are the lead NGOs.

Sri Lanka

Two flagship Resplendent Ceylon lodges (Cape Weligama on the south coast, Wild Coast Tented Lodge at Yala). Lead marine NGOs are Blue Resources Trust (research and policy) and Pearl Protectors (community).

British Indian Ocean Territory (Chagos)

The BIOT Marine Protected Area (gazetted 2010) is one of the largest no-take marine reserves in the world at 640,000 km². No commercial premium lodging operates there given the Diego Garcia military presence, but the Bertarelli Foundation and Zoological Society of London run long-running coral and reef-fish science programmes.

Lakshadweep (India)

Limited 5-star tourism infrastructure but globally significant atoll reef. Reef Watch Marine Conservation runs the lead reef monitoring programme.

Mozambique

Anantara Bazaruto, &Beyond Benguerra, and Vamizi Island Lodge cover Bazaruto and Quirimbas archipelagos. Bazaruto National Park (since 1971) is one of the most important dugong strongholds remaining anywhere. Quirimbas National Park (since 2002) covers both terrestrial and marine. Lead NGOs: WWF Mozambique, Bazaruto Centre for Scientific Studies, Marine Megafauna Foundation (Tofo Beach).

Madagascar

Time + Tide Miavana on Nosy Ankao is the flagship ultra-luxury island lodge. Blue Ventures runs the Velondriake LMMA, a 1,000 km² community-managed marine area in southwest Madagascar that has become a global LMMA reference case. Wildlife Conservation Society Madagascar runs national marine programmes.

Tanzania and Zanzibar

&Beyond Mnemba Island is the flagship private-island lodge, working alongside the Mnemba Atoll Marine Sanctuary. Sea Sense Tanzania runs the national sea turtle and dugong conservation programme.

What is missing from this page, and why

  • Comoros. Limited premium-eco infrastructure currently. May be added in a future pass.
  • Réunion. French overseas territory with active reef science but limited premium-eco island lodges. National Marine Park exists but resort coverage is mainland-style.
  • Andaman Islands (India). Significant reef + restoration story, but premium-eco infrastructure is limited. Future addition.

For trip planning purposes, contact each property and programme directly to confirm operating status, season, and specific volunteer or scientific access. This page is a reference, not a booking engine.
