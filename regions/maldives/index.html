Region reference, Indian Ocean

Maldives: premium-eco lodges and reef restoration.

Twenty-eight verified premium-eco lodges across nine atolls, paired with on-site or partner reef restoration programmes. Plus the Hanifaru Bay UNESCO Biosphere Reserve and the largest concentration of resort-led coral restoration anywhere.

The short version. The Maldives has the world's densest concentration of resort-led marine restoration. Reefscapers operates in 30+ resorts. Marine Savers anchors at all four Four Seasons properties. Manta Trust runs from Hanifaru Bay (UNESCO Biosphere since 2011). Soneva Foundation, Olive Ridley Project, Banyan Tree Marine Lab, and the Maldives Whale Shark Research Programme add further depth. This page is the working reference for which lodge does what.

The atolls

The Maldives is 26 natural atolls organised into 20 administrative atolls. Premium-eco resorts cluster across nine of them. From north to south: Haa Dhaalu (Soneva Secret), Raa (Joali, Joali Being), Lhaviyani (Six Senses Kanuhura), Noonu (Soneva Jani, Cheval Blanc Randheli, Velaa), Baa (Soneva Fushi, Anantara Kihavah, Four Seasons Landaa Giraavaru, Four Seasons Voavah), North Malé (Patina, Ritz-Carlton Fari, One&Only Reethi Rah, Four Seasons Kuda Huraa, Gili Lankanfushi, Huvafen Fushi, Coco Privé), South Malé (Anantara Veli, Naladhu, COMO Cocoa Island, Waldorf Astoria Ithaafushi), Ari (W Maldives, Conrad Rangali), Dhaalu (Niyama, St Regis Vommuli), Thaa (COMO Maalifushi), and Laamu (Six Senses Laamu).

Hanifaru Bay UNESCO Biosphere Reserve

Baa Atoll was inscribed as a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve in 2011 [1]. Its core zone, Hanifaru Bay, hosts one of the largest known seasonal aggregations of reef manta rays (Mobula alfredi) and supports whale shark visits during the southwest monsoon (May to November). Visitor access is regulated, with guides accredited via the Manta Trust [2]. Baa Atoll resorts including Soneva Fushi, Anantara Kihavah, Four Seasons Landaa Giraavaru, and Four Seasons Voavah operate within the buffer and transition zones of the biosphere.

Resort-led restoration: who runs what

Reefscapers

Founded in 2001 by Marine Savers' Thomas Le Berre, Reefscapers operates coral propagation in 30+ Maldivian resorts using sponsored coral frames [3]. Their flagship lab is at Four Seasons Landaa Giraavaru in Baa Atoll. Frames are out-planted by guests as part of programmed activities.

Marine Savers (Four Seasons Maldives)

Marine Savers operates across both Four Seasons resorts (Landaa Giraavaru in Baa, Kuda Huraa in North Malé) plus the Voavah private island [4]. Programmes include: a marine turtle rehabilitation centre at Landaa Giraavaru with capacity for ten resident turtles, a manta-ray ID database that has logged over 5,500 individuals, and tagged whale shark research in collaboration with the Maldives Whale Shark Research Programme.

Soneva Foundation Make Waves

The Soneva Foundation funds reef-restoration plus waste programmes across Soneva resorts (Fushi, Jani, Secret) [5]. Make Waves is the foundation's coral and waste pillar, with mineral accretion (Biorock-style) installations at Soneva Fushi.

Manta Trust

Manta Trust is the world's lead reef-manta NGO, founded 2011, headquartered in the Maldives at Baa Atoll. Their flagship Maldivian Manta Ray Project has documented over 5,000 individual reef manta rays, the world's largest known population [2]. Manta Trust accredits Hanifaru Bay guides and partners with multiple resort-based citizen science programmes.

Maldives Whale Shark Research Programme (MWSRP)

MWSRP is the principal NGO researching whale sharks (Rhincodon typus) in Maldivian waters since 2006 [6]. Their core area is South Ari MPA. Partner resorts include Conrad Maldives Rangali (within the MPA boundary) and others on Ari Atoll.

Olive Ridley Project

Olive Ridley Project is the Indian Ocean's lead sea turtle NGO. They operate turtle rehabilitation centres at multiple Maldives resorts plus a research arm focused on ghost net entanglement, with the largest dataset of olive ridley turtle entanglements in the Indian Ocean [7].

Banyan Tree Marine Lab Vabbinfaru

One of the longest-running resort-funded marine labs in the Maldives, established 2002 at Banyan Tree Vabbinfaru in North Malé Atoll. Coral propagation lab plus turtle research [8].

Honest gaps

  • Climate-bleaching pressure. The Maldives experienced a major bleaching event in 1998 and another in 2016. Restoration programmes alone cannot solve the underlying climate problem. Most resort-led restoration explicitly frames its role as supplementary to mitigation.
  • Single-use plastic and waste. Even at premium-eco resorts, supply chains include long-haul food and beverage imports. Soneva Foundation's Make Waves explicitly addresses waste; many resorts have limited public reporting.
  • Not every resort that brands itself eco runs a verified programme. The list above is restricted to programmes with on-site staff and traceable outputs.
