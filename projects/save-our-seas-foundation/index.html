Project profile, Seychelles

Save Our Seas Foundation.

D'Arros Research Centre in the Seychelles Amirantes, Aldabra-aligned work, and a global research-grant programme that has funded over 400 marine science projects in 80+ countries.

The short version. The Save Our Seas Foundation (SOSF) is a Geneva-headquartered international NGO that funds shark, ray, and chimaera science globally. It owns and operates the SOSF D'Arros Research Centre in the Seychelles Amirantes, runs the Shark Education Centre in South Africa, and has provided seed funding for 400+ marine research projects globally [1].

D'Arros Research Centre

D'Arros Island and its sister atoll St Joseph (the Amirantes group, Outer Islands) are the SOSF's research base. Long-running tagging studies on lemon, blacktip, and sicklefin lemon sharks; reef-fish surveys; turtle nesting monitoring; manta and mobula sightings [1] [2]. The centre operates under government concession and is closed to general tourism but partners with research-permitted yacht charters.

Aldabra-aligned work

SOSF supports research that contributes to the Aldabra Atoll UNESCO World Heritage knowledge base [3]. Direct site management of Aldabra is held by the Seychelles Islands Foundation (SIF); SOSF funds species-level research on resident sharks and rays and on the largest free-roaming Aldabra giant tortoise population on Earth.

Global grants programme

SOSF runs an annual call for research, education, and conservation grants targeting elasmobranch (sharks, rays, chimaeras) and broader marine science. Grant recipients span 80+ countries, with funded projects published openly on the foundation's site [1].

Governance and funding

SOSF is a private foundation funded primarily by its founder. Annual reports detail grant amounts, partner organisations, and outcomes [1]. Independent audited financials are published.

How visitors engage

Direct visits to D'Arros are restricted to research-permitted vessels. The Mahé-based head office welcomes professional researchers and accredited media. For the general visitor, the SOSF's open data portal and published research are the primary engagement points.
